Is nucular or nuclear pronounced?

Nucular is a rough phonetic spelling of /ˈnjuːkjələr/, the commonly prescribed-against pronunciation of the word “nuclear“.

How is the word nuclear pronounced?

The word “nuclear” is pronounced differently in different English-speaking regions of the world.” In the United States, the word is pronounced “NEW-klee-er” with the emphasis on the first syllable. However, in the United Kingdom, the word is pronounced “New-KLEE-ah” with the emphasis on the second syllable.

Is nuclear a word?

Nuclear describes something that has to do with the nucleus — the central part — of an atom. The word nuclear is sometimes used broadly to refer to the core of something. Your nuclear family is you, your parents, and your siblings — in a sense, the central part of your world, kind of like the nucleus of an atom.

What does nuclear waste look like?

The key component of nuclear waste is the leftover smaller nuclei, known as fission products. The fission process of a single atomic nucleus. From the outside, nuclear waste looks exactly like the fuel that was loaded into the reactor — typically assemblies of cylindrical metal rods enclosing fuel pellets.

What do Nuclear mean?

The definition of nuclear is relating to the nucleus of a cell, or relating to atomic or hydrogen bombs. An example of nuclear used as an adjective is the phrase “nuclear energy” which means energy that comes from the nucleus of a cell.

What a nuclear bomb does?

Modern nuclear weapons work by combining chemical explosives, nuclear fission, and nuclear fusion. The explosives compress nuclear material, causing fission; the fission releases massive amounts of energy in the form of X-rays, which create the high temperature and pressure needed to ignite fusion.

Can you survive a nuclear bomb?

Today’s nuclear weapons are devastating nightmares, but people can and do survive even when they are close to the bomb’s blast radius. Japanese man Tsutomu Yamaguchi lived through the bombings of both Hiroshima and Nagasaki and died at the age of 93.

How does a nuclear bomb eliminate you?

An explosion that is farther above the Earth’s surface than the radius of the fireball does not dig a crater and produces negligible immediate fallout. For the most part, a nuclear blast kills people by indirect means rather than by direct pressure.

How far away can a nuclear bomb eliminate you?

Death is highly likely and radiation poisoning is almost certain if one is caught in the open with no terrain or building masking effects within a radius of 0–3 km from a 1 megaton airburst, and the 50% chance of death from the blast extends out to ~8 km from the same 1 megaton atmospheric explosion.
