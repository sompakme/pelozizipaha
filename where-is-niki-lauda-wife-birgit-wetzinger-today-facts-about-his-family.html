Career

Niki pulled out a bank loan in 1971 to purchase his way into the Formula Two circuit, and he was quickly promoted to the Formula One squad. In 1973, the racer borrowed money from a bank to join the British Racing Motors (BRM) squad. When he raced in that year’s Monaco Grand Prix, he piqued Enzo Ferrari’s interest and joined the Ferrari team in 1974. 

He finished second in his debut race for Ferrari, the Argentine Grand Prix, and won the Spanish Grand Prix the following year. In 1975, Lauda won four races in the new Ferrari 312T, then finished third in the Italian Grand Prix and first in the US Grand Prix.

He said during practice for the 1979 Canadian Grand Prix that he was no longer interested in continuing “the folly of going around in circles” and would be quitting immediately. After being promised $3 million pay to join the McLaren team, Niki came out of retirement in 1982.

He won the 1982 Long Beach Grand Prix and the 1984 Monaco Grand Prix before breaking his wrist during the 1985 Belgian Grand Prix practice.

He announced his retirement from racing at the end of the 1985 season. After retiring, Niki had non-racing responsibilities with many Formula One teams, including a consultancy post with the Ferrari team in 1993 and a non-executive chairman role with the Mercedes AMG Petronas team in 2012.

He was elected into the International Motorsports Hall of Fame in 1993, and in 2005, the Austrian post office recognized him with a stamp. He was named number 22 on ESPN.com’s list of the greatest 25 drivers of all time in 2008.
