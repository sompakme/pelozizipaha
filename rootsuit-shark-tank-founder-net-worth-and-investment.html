However, the sharks expressed confusion about the functionality of RootSuit and how it differed from other swimsuits on the market. As a result, they declined to invest, leaving Colin Grussing without a deal.

At the time of the pitch, RootSuit had a net worth of $660,000, based on the offer that was made for the equity stake. Despite not receiving a deal on Shark Tank, RootSuit’s appearance on the show brought increased visibility and attention to the brand.

RootSuit Pitch on Shark Tank

During their Shark Tank pitch, Colin Grussing showcased RootSuit by bringing a dance troupe wearing the suits. The sharks, including Robert Herjavec, Lori Greiner, Kevin O’Leary, and Daymond John, expressed concerns about competition and declined to invest. Ultimately, Grussing did not secure a deal on the show.

Despite the vibrant display and unique concept of RootSuit, the sharks were hesitant due to the saturated market of spandex bodysuits and the lack of clear differentiation from other swimwear products. The sharks saw potential for competition to easily replicate the product and questioned its longevity in the market.

Kevin O’Leary voiced his concerns, stating, “I’m not convinced that this is a lasting business. It’s just a fad that could easily be replaced by other similar products.”

Although the sharks did not see the investment opportunity in RootSuit, the pitch on Shark Tank provided valuable exposure for the brand, leading to increased visibility and recognition.

RootSuit Founder

Colin Grussing, a mechanical engineer and athlete, is the visionary behind RootSuit. In 2012, Grussing founded RootSuit with a mission to revolutionize the world of swimwear. Drawing from his own experiences with the discomfort of swimwear drag, he set out to create a unique and innovative solution that would enhance performance and style.

Driven by his passion for engineering and sports, Grussing designed a skin-tight full-body neon suit that would not only reduce drag but also make a bold fashion statement. The RootSuit became a symbol of empowerment and self-expression, combining functionality and aesthetics in one sleek package.

“I wanted to create a swimwear solution that would enhance performance and allow individuals to stand out from the crowd. RootSuit is the perfect fusion of form and function, bringing joy and confidence to those who wear it,”

RootSuit After Shark Tank

After their appearance on Shark Tank, RootSuit experienced a significant boost in visibility and sales. The exposure from the show helped create awareness about the brand and its unique neon full-body suits.

“Being on Shark Tank was a game-changer for us. The national television exposure opened doors to new opportunities and customers,”

The company capitalized on their newfound attention by launching a new line of products. Building on the success of their original RootSuit, they expanded their range to include different colors and designs to cater to a wider market.

The RootSuit team also aimed to strengthen their distribution network and make their products more accessible to customers across the country. They secured partnerships with various retail locations, allowing shoppers to try on and purchase the suits in-store.
