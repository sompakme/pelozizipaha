The Invention of Safety Nailer

It was a collaboration between Drew Zirkle and his friend Daniel Eifes that led to the invention of the revolutionary Safety Nailer. This innovative tool is designed to prevent injuries and enhance safety during nail and hammer usage. The Safety Nailer is a finger guard equipped with a magnetic mechanism that securely holds the nail in place, ensuring precise and controlled strikes while eliminating the risk of accidental impacts.

What sets the Safety Nailer apart is its construction. Crafted from durable and impact-resistant hard plastic, this finger guard is built to withstand even the most forceful strikes. Its rigid material ensures that it retains its shape, providing consistent protection and durability over time.

With the Safety Nailer, users no longer have to worry about misaligned swings or painful accidents. The magnetic mechanism firmly secures the nail, ensuring it remains in position throughout the hammering process. This innovation prevents the nail from slipping or falling, significantly reducing the risk of injuries caused by wayward strikes.

The Founders of Safety Nailer Framer

Drew Zirkle and Kristen Eifes are the visionary founders behind Safety Nailer Framer. However, the journey of creating this innovative safety tool began with a collaboration between Daniel Eifes, Kristen’s husband, and Drew, who were college friends.

Daniel, with Kristen’s invaluable input, designed and patented the Safety Nailer – a device that provides finger protection and prevents injuries while using a nail and hammer. Unfortunately, Daniel’s promising involvement in the business was cut short when he was medically discharged from the Navy due to a medical condition. Nevertheless, his determination to create a solution to protect users led to the birth of Safety Nailer Framer.

With Daniel’s invention as their inspiration, Drew and Kristen came together and embarked on a mission to bring the Safety Nailer to the market. Their shared passion for safety and their ability to work cohesively as a team allowed them to overcome challenges and turn their vision into reality.

Safety Nailer Framer Shark Tank Pitch

In his compelling pitch on Shark Tank, Drew Zirkle presented Safety Nailer Framer, seeking a $100,000 investment for 14% equity in the company. He valuated Safety Nailer Framer at an impressive $714,286. And while the Sharks initially expressed some reservations about the product’s viability and the business model, it didn’t take long for their interest to be piqued.

“When I see a young man like you come into the Shark Tank, putting all of your energy behind this invention, you can see the passion in your eyes,” remarked Lori Greiner, one of the esteemed Shark Tank investors.

Ultimately, Mark Cuban, Lori Greiner, and Rohan Oza were convinced of the potential of Safety Nailer Framer, offering Drew Zirkle the desired $100,000 investment. In exchange, they sought a 33.3% equity stake in the company, a deal that Drew eagerly accepted.

With the Sharks on board, Safety Nailer Framer embarked on a journey towards success, capitalizing on the exposure and expertise brought by the investment.
