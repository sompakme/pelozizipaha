Johnny Knoxville's Net Worth: How A Former Extra Turned Stunts Into Millions

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, September 15, 2024

Highlights

  • Johnny Knoxville's determination for success led to a breakthrough after years of hard work and persistence in the entertainment industry.
  • A single idea for a show with friends turned Knoxville into a household name, as the star of the iconic series Jackass.
  • From stunts to acting, Knoxville's versatility and dedication to his craft have helped him achieve an estimated net worth of $50 million today.

In 1989, a handsome young man named Philip John Clapp, then 18, moved to California from Knoxville. His dream was simple: he wanted to become an actor.

But soon, reality sunk in for him — he would have to work much harder to stand out and become a mainstream actor.

Johnny Knoxville accepted this challenge and began to accept roles as an extra to get out there, and while doing so, he sought a breakthrough.

Eight long years passed, but Knoxville had no luck — he endeavored to knock on every door, and wrote and pitched articles to nearly every magazine, in a serious effort to show the entertainment industry what he was capable of.

A young man who dreamed of becoming an actor when he was a teen finally got his chance at the age of 27, with skateboarding magazine Big Brother's video "Number Two," in which he tested skateboards as a stuntman.

With that, Knoxville's journey as a youthful extra ended, and a new stuntman emerged — but still, fame was a few years ahead on the horizon.

Today, let’s look at Johnny Knoxville's net worth, how he changed his fate with one idea, how he became a massively popular personality in entertainment, and how Knoxville built a massive fortune.

One 'Jackass' Idea Changed Johnny Knoxville's Whole Life

After 13 years of toiling in the entertainment industry, Johnny Knoxville still hadn't managed to land a single, short role in a major movie.

Knoxville was swimming in a sea of anonymity, but a lifeboat – in the form of a life-changing idea – would soon emerge to carry him to the shores of success.

Jeff Tremaine was a notable editor for the influential skateboarding magazine Big Brother — and he was the person who liked Johnny Knoxville's skateboard testing idea, and featured him in an uncredited role in Big Brother's "Number Two" video in 1998.

After a year went by, Knoxville once again sought out Tremaine with an idea. This time around, Johnny would strike gold.

Along with Spike Jonze, Knoxville and Tremaine developed something new, a show instantly recognizable to a generation of MTV viewers — the infamous and controversial series Jackass.

Jackass was a reality comedy series, based on the crazy stunts and mischief of nine friends: Bam Margera, the late Ryan Dunn, Steve-O, Jason "Wee Man" Acuña, Dave England, Ehren McGhehey, Chris Pontius, Preston Lacy, and their de facto leader, Johnny Knoxville:

The Jackass crew conducted outlandish stunts and pranks, and they were all so skillful that the show was an instant success, making them superstars overnight.

Although the Jackass series wrapped after three years, by that time, Johnny Knoxville was in demand, thoroughly enjoying the fame and fortune he'd dreamed of 13 years prior.

Jackass was over, but the public was definitively not "over" Jackass' wild (and often dangerous) stunts — so the Jackass cast decided to make a movie.

In 2002, Jackass: The Movie was released, and audiences welcomed this film with open arms. On a scant $5 million budget, Jackass: The Movie grossed $79.5 million.

In other words, a 20 times over profit meant Jackass was a big franchise, and its newly minted stars did everything in their power to make it even bigger.

They made spinoffs and sequels of the movies, and launched games. They also sold unreleased material, created live blogs and websites containing stunts, and sold merchandise.

Jackass was the key to Johnny Knoxville's entrance into the mainstream movie sphere, launching him into a new journey.

Johnny Knoxville: Jack Of All Trades, Jackass Of None

After 13 years of grinding in the entertainment industry, Johnny Knoxville still had yet to land a significant role in a major film.

However, 2002 was the year that changed Knoxville's life.

Prior to his breakthrough in 2002, Johnny's acting projects consisted of short roles and brief video appearances.

But after the huge success of Jackass in 2002, in a single year, he worked on Big Trouble and Men In Black 2 as a major character, and scored a minor role in Wild Deuces.

Knoxville wanted to prove his mettle as an actor from the beginning, and he was finally making his dreams come true.

He showcased his acting skills in several great movies, like Grand Theft Parsons, Walking Tall, The Ringer, The Last Stand, Bad Grandpa, Elvis & Nixon, and Above Suspicion.

Throughout his acting career, Knoxville tackled nearly every kind of role: serious roles, comedy roles, voice acting, stunts, and background actor.

There are 55 major acting and voice acting projects to his credit, as well as 29 producer credits, and 16 projects on which he wrote.

Apparently, Johnny Knoxville is the proverbial "Jack Of All Trades," and he is also a master of all of them.
