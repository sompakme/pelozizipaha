Pharmaceutical companies are involved in pharmaceutical drug research, development, manufacturing, and commercialization. They play a crucial role in healthcare by discovering and producing new medicines that treat, prevent, or manage various diseases and medical conditions. These companies work around the clock to combat emerging infections affecting the world, such as the COVID 19 virus. Pharmaceutical companies make money by investing in research and development to discover new drugs and health products that give them a competitive advantage in the market. Pharmaceutical corporations maintain a diversified portfolio of marketed products that generate huge sales and profits. Effective marketing methods, vast distribution networks, and strong partnerships with healthcare professionals all contribute to successful drug sales, which total billions of dollars.

AstraZeneca plc –$44.4 billion

AstraZeneca plc is a multinational pharmaceutical company situated in the United Kingdom. It is one of the largest pharmaceutical companies globally, specializing in researching, developing, and manufacturing prescription medicines in various therapeutic areas. AstraZeneca's success can be attributed to several factors, including its strong research and development capabilities. The company invests significant revenue into research and development, allowing it to discover and develop new therapies and medications.

Bristol-Myers Squibb –$46.2 billion

Via Flickr/mmutzel

Bristol-Myers Squibb (BMS) is a global pharmaceutical company headquartered in the United States. It is one of the world's big pharmaceutical corporations, focusing in prescription medication research, development, and commercialization. In addition to its focus on research and development, it has a portfolio of marketed drugs that contribute to its financial success. Bristol-Myers Squibb's financial performance and reputation are founded on the company's commitment to scientific excellence, patient-centeredness, and a commitment to improve healthcare globally.

Hoffmann-La Roche AG –$50 billion

Roche, or Hoffmann-La Roche AG, is a global pharmaceutical business located in Switzerland. It is one of the largest pharmaceutical companies in the world, with a strong focus on healthcare diagnostics and pharmaceuticals. As a leading pharmaceutical company, Roche invests heavily in research and development to drive innovation and advance medical knowledge. The company's financial success is due to its commitment on producing high-quality healthcare products, investing in research and development, and maintaining a strong global presence.

Novartis International AG –$50 billion

Novartis International AG, commonly known as Novartis, is a Swiss multinational pharmaceutical company based in Basel, Switzerland. It focuses on research, development, manufacturing, and commercialization of a wide range of pharmaceutical products. The innovative medicines subdivision of the corporation is focused to identifying and developing novel therapeutics for a variety of therapeutic drugs. They actively engage in strategic partnerships, collaborations, and acquisitions to enhance its product offerings and expand its presence in different markets around the world.

Merck & Co., Inc. –$52.05 billion

Via Flickr/Kate Hannon

Merck & Co., Inc., or Merck, is a global pharmaceutical firm established in the United States. Merck focuses on the development, production, and marketing of novel prescription medications, vaccines, and animal health products. Merck has expanded its emphasis beyond human health to include animal health. The company develops and manufactures a range of veterinary medicines and vaccines to improve the health and well-being of animals.

Johnson & Johnson –$52.60 billion

Via Flickr/Mayo Clinic

Johnson & Johnson is a multinational healthcare company based in the United States. It is one of the largest and most diversified pharmaceutical, medical device, and consumer goods manufacturers in the world. Johnson & Johnson has three major business segments: pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and consumer health. Johnson & Johnson's success may be traced back to the company's global dedication to scientific innovation, patient care, and social responsibility.

AbbVie Inc –$58.00 billion

AbbVie Inc. is a multinational pharmaceutical company based in the United States. It split off from Abbott Laboratories in 2013 as a separate company with a focus on pharmaceutical product research, development, manufacturing, and commercialization. Its financial success may be attributed to its capacity to develop and sell new drugs, high brand awareness, and successful marketing strategies. The company's focus on meeting unmet medical needs and improving patient outcomes has helped it become a prominent player in the pharmaceutical industry.

Pfizer Inc –$100 billion

Via Flickr/Waleed Alzuhair

Pfizer Inc. is a multinational pharmaceutical corporation situated in the United States. It is one of the largest pharmaceutical companies in the world, specializing in the discovery, development, manufacturing, and marketing of prescription drugs and vaccines. Pfizer's financial success is due to its diverse product range, worldwide presence, and strategic alliances.
