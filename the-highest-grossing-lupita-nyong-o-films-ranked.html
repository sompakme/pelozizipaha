The Highest-Grossing Lupita Nyong'o Films, Ranked

Lupita Nyong'o is a Kenyan-Mexican actress born in Mexico City on March 1, 1983. Lupita Nyong'o is a gifted actress who has built a name for herself in Hollywood, winning critical praise and box office success in a surprisingly short period. She is best known for her critically acclaimed performances in films such as 12 Years a Slave (2013), for which she won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress, and Us (2019).

Nyong'o is also actively involved in humanitarian work as a global ambassador for the anti-poverty organization CARE and an advocate for the Africa Cancer Foundation. She has been recognized for her contributions to the arts and humanitarian causes, receiving awards such as the Essence, Black Women in Hollywood Breakthrough Performance Award, and the Maui Film Festival Rainmaker Award.Sit back and relax as we take a look at 10 of Lupita Nyong'o's highest-grossing films.

Little Monsters (2019) - $2.3 million

Little Monsters is a 2019 horror-comedy starring Nyong'o as kindergarten teacher Miss Caroline. Despite conflicting reviews, critics complimented Nyong'o's ability to balance the film's humorous and tragic themes. Little Monsters is worth watching for horror-comedy fans and Nyong'o fans.

The Queen of Katwe (2016) - $10.4 million

The Queen of Katwe, a 2016 biographical movie about Phiona Mutesi, a poor Ugandan child who becomes a chess champion. Harriet, Phiona's mother, is played by Nyong'o. Nyong'o's performance was lauded. Biographical drama enthusiasts should watch The Queen of Katwe charming and uplifting tale of tenacity and determination that under-performed at the box office.

The Jungle Book: Mowgli's Story (1998) - $24.5 million

Via Wikimedia Commons

The Jungle Book: Mowgli's Tale is a 1998 live-action picture that retells the classic story from Mowgli's perspective. Nyong'o voices Raksha, a wolf who adopts Mowgli as her son. Nyong'o's voice acting gave her role depth and empathy, despite the film's negative reviews. The Jungle Book: Mowgli's Tale is a sentimental trip down memory lane for Disney fans and a chance to hear Nyong'o's voice acting.

12 Years an enslaved person (2013) - $187.7 million

12 Years a Slave is a 2013 historical drama film based on the true story of Solomon Northup, a free Black man from New York who was kidnapped and sold into slavery in the antebellum South. Nyong'o plays Patsey, an enslaved person subjected to cruel treatment by her master. The film was critically acclaimed and won multiple prizes, including the Oscar Award for Best Picture. Nyong'o's performance was similarly lauded, with many considering it her breakthrough role. 12 Years a Slave depicts a dark period in American history in a compelling and distressing way.

Non-Stop (2014) - $222.8 million

Non-Stop is an action-thriller featuring Liam Neeson as a US air marshal seeking to halt a transatlantic hijacking. Gwen, played by Nyong'o, helps Neeson's character investigate. Nyong'o was lauded for humanizing her modest role. Non-Stop grossed $222 million globally despite mixed reviews. Although it's not Nyong'o's most memorable performance, she provides dimension to the ensemble group.

Us (2019) - $255.1 million

Us is a 2019 horror movie written and directed by Jordan Peele. Nyong'o plays Adelaide Wilson, a woman who returns to her childhood home with her family, only to find a group of counterparts waiting for them and planning to kill them. The movie received good reviews, and Nyong'o's performance was praised for showing weakness and strength. The film also did well at the box office, grossing over $255 million worldwide. Fans of horror movies and people who like Nyong'o's work should see this movie.

The Jungle Book (2016) - $966.6 million

The Jungle Book is a 2016 live-action film that follows Mowgli, a jungle kid raised by animals. Nyong'o voices mother wolf Raksha, who adopts Mowgli. It grossed approximately $966.6 million worldwide. Its breathtaking visuals and faithful reproduction of the original plot were lauded. Many appreciated Nyong'o's voice acting for giving her character heart. The timeless story and stunning visual effects make The Jungle Book a must-see.

Star Wars: The Last Jedi (2017) - $1.333 billion

Star Wars: The Last Jedi is a 2017 American epic space opera film directed and written by Rian Johnson. It is the eighth installment in the Star Wars franchise and the second film in the sequel trilogy, following The Force Awakens (2015). Nyong'o plays Resistance ally Maz Kanata, a former pirate. The film grossed $1.333 billion worldwide and was well-received. Nyong'o was lauded for her character's complexity and humor despite her brief screen time.

Black Panther (2018) - $1.346 billion

A 2018 superhero movie, Black Panther, is set in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. The film has an all-star ensemble, and Nyong'o plays Nakia, a Wakandan spy and T'Challa's past love interest. The movie won widespread acclaim for its innovative portrayal of black culture and ideas, powerful performances, and spectacular imagery. It became one of the highest-grossing movies after achieving a worldwide gross of nearly $1.346 billion. Fans of superhero flicks and people who value original works of art should not miss Black Panther.

Star Wars: The Force Awakens (2015) - $2.07 billion

Star Wars: The Force Awakens is a 2015 American epic space opera film directed by J.J. Abrams and written by Abrams, Lawrence Kasdan, and Michael Arndt. Nyong'o plays Maz Kanata, a knowledgeable old alien who aids the film's heroes. The anticipated picture grossed over $2.07 billion worldwide. It was lauded for its nostalgia and diverse cast. Many complimented Nyong'o's Maz Kanata portrayal for her wisdom and humor. Star Wars: The Force Awakens is a must-see for franchise fans and sci-fi moviegoers.
